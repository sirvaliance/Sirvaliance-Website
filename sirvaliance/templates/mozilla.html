The Crypto Project

I founded The Crypto Project and it officially launched on July 15. From the website, “The Crypto Project is an organization designed to assist and encourage anonymity and encryption research, development, and use”. The project has 4 goals:

  • Building a website that becomes an authoritative, reliable, and current resource for those learning how to achieve privacy, security, and anonymity.
  • Bringing the security, cryptography, and development communities together to work on projects, discuss ideas, and unite to create progress.
  • Build a large network of anonymity, privacy, and censorship circumvention services.
  • Create, assist in creating, promote, and help others use software to achieve privacy, security, and anonymity.

My Work on The Crypto Project

As the founder, I orginally conceived the idea and designed and built out the website within the span of a day (when I work, I work fast). I originally built the site using Tornado, but once it became clear we needed a wiki for user contributed content I devised a different architecture. For visual design and security reasons, I was hesitant to use any of the wiki software out there and decided our best option was to create a repository of Markdown templates. I forked and modified Gollum for our Wiki, and subsequently built (hacked together is a better term) a static site generator called Hoggle to build both our site and blog. All of our server builds and updates are automated and managed through Fabric Scripts. I am passionate and a strong believer in automating anything that can be automated.

As well as writing and setting up our website, I have set up and work as an administrator to our Network. In our network, I have setup and operate a Tor Relay, a Mixminion Relay, a Convergence Notary, a TorProject.org Mirror, and have begun work on a Tahoe-LAFS grid behind Tor hidden services. All of our services run behind an Nginx reverse SSL proxy and we like to implement secure features like HSTS.

I am a passionate developer who spends my day's working on open-source code, talking to hacker friends on IRC, and working as an activist for open source software and knowledge, particularly related to privacy, anonymity, and security. I would love to bring the passion and talent exemplified in current work to Mozilla!
